# Ping

Ping is a lightweight social coordination platform built around groups.

It’s designed for one thing:

> Turning a quick thought into coordinated action — instantly.

---

## 🧠 What is Ping?

Ping replaces slow, noisy communication with fast, actionable interactions.

Instead of opening a chat, writing messages, waiting for replies, and losing context —  
you just create a **Ping**.

Examples:

- “Who’s in for lunch?”
- “Anyone driving to Town Center?”
- “Need a charger”
- “FIFA?”

People respond. You act. Done.

---

## 👥 Groups First

Ping is built around **groups**.

Each group is a focused social space:

- Friends
- Work teams
- Roommates
- Communities

Everything happens inside groups, so context is always clear.

---

## ⚡ How It Works

1. Create or join a group
2. Open a Ping (a short request)
3. Others respond instantly
4. You coordinate and move on

No threads. No clutter. No overthinking.

---

## 🔑 Core Concepts

### Ping

A short-lived, action-oriented request.

- Clear intent
- Fast responses
- Temporary by nature

---

### Group

A shared space where people interact.

Groups control:

- Who can join
- Who can invite others
- Who can create pings

---

### Membership

Each user has a role inside a group:

- Owner
- Admin
- Member

Permissions are simple and flexible.

---

## 🎯 Product Philosophy

Ping is intentionally **not**:

- A chat app
- A social network
- A task manager

Ping is:

> A coordination layer between people.

---

## ⚖️ Design Principles

- **Speed over completeness**  
  Creating a Ping should take seconds.

- **Signal over noise**  
  Every interaction should lead to action.

- **Context by default**  
  Groups eliminate ambiguity.

- **Short-lived interactions**  
  Pings are not meant to live forever.

---

## 🚀 Vision

Ping starts with small groups and simple coordination.

Long term, it can evolve into:

- Real-time coordination across larger communities
- Location-based interactions
- Smart suggestions and matching
- A new way to organize spontaneous activity

---

## 🧩 The Problem

Today, coordination looks like this:

- Messages get buried
- People reply late
- Context is lost
- Decisions take too long

Ping fixes that by making coordination:

> Immediate, visible, and actionable.

---

## 🧭 Why It Matters

People don’t need more communication tools.

They need:

> Less friction between intention and action.

Ping exists to remove that friction.
